The ingredients needed to make Masala Beef with Ginger and Curry Leaf:

  • Make ready 3 bay leaves
  • Take 1 (1 inch) piece cinnamon stick
  • Make ready 5 cardamom pods
  • Take 4 whole cloves
  • Get 2 teaspoons fennel seeds
  • Make ready 10 whole black peppercorns
  • Make ready 2 pounds beef tenderloin, cubed
  • Take 3 cups chopped onion, divided
  • Take 5 green chile peppers, halved lengthwise
  • Get 1 (1 1/2 inch) piece fresh ginger root, grated
  • Take 6 cloves garlic, minced
  • Get ½ teaspoon ground turmeric
  • Make ready 1 teaspoon salt
  • Take ½ cup coconut oil
  • Make ready ¼ teaspoon whole mustard seeds
  • Take 4 fresh curry leaves
  • Get 2 ½ teaspoons lemon juice
  • Take 1 teaspoon ground black pepper

Instructions to cook Masala Beef with Ginger and Curry Leaf:

  1. To make the masala powder: Grind the bay leaves, cinnamon, cardamom, cloves, fennel seeds and peppercorns in a spice grinder until mixture is a fine powder.
  2. Place the beef cubes, masala powder, 2 cups chopped onion, green chiles, grated fresh ginger, garlic and turmeric in a large, heavy pot. Add water to cover (about 1 cup) and bring to a boil. Reduce heat and simmer for 30 minutes until beef is cooked through. Add salt. Stir and continue to simmer about 10 minutes or until mixture is almost dry, but do not allow it to burn (add a bit more water, if necessary). Set aside.
  3. Heat oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add mustard seeds and cook until they begin to pop. Immediately add remaining 1 cup chopped onion and stir over medium heat until onions soften and begin to brown, about 10 to 12 minutes. Add curry leaves and cook until brown, about 3 minutes.
  4. Stir in the beef mixture, black pepper, and lemon juice. Cook until nicely browned and heated through, about 8 minutes.

Here are some tips though: 1) Cube your beef really small (like 1/2 inch pieces) so that you'll get more flavor onto your meat. 2) Instead of lemon juice use 1 tomato chopped up to add a little acidity. Be sure to cook it down all the way. 3) You can use about 1-2 tsp of red chili powder as per your taste (ie. cayenne powder NOT chili powder for chili) instead of the green chili peppers or use a smaller amount of both. They have different flavors but both provide heat. 4) Don't fry your onions until crisp...just sautee until slightly brown if you want the flavors & masala to penetrate the meat. 5) You can pre-cook your meat with ginger onions salt a little turmeric and green chilis in a pressure cooker to make the beef more tender.
